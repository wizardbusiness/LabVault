RIICH is my solution to frustrations with the state and status of documentation within the industry today. It's a documentation framework for writing documentation that's a stateful representation of an organizations vital processes and tools,  a reference and how-to repository, and a multiplier for deepening and normalizing organization-level knowledge. Essentially I wanted something that is designed with the needs of everyone on the team in mind, from interns to veteran engineers, and that desire is responsible for many of the core design decisions behind RIICH. Another source of influence is my desire for tooling that leverages and reinforces my existing experience writing high-quality, structured documentation, while taking advantage of AI technologies to enhance rather than replace individual and organization-level know-how. Simple right? :)

Structurally RIICH's architecture echoes the design of a Unix pipeline. Each doc unit does one thing well, outputs something another doc can consume as input, and the whole structure stays legible and predictable. The key raw ingredients for this system, as with any functional documentation system, are human wisdom, attention, and bespoke systems-knowledge. RIICH reinforces this core of institutional vitality, while building richer systemic context and discoverability using scoped AI tooling solutions, and designing the user interface for various types of documentation usecases. For example, one person might be looking at the docs for an internal tool to answer a quick question about how to accomplish a specific task, like printing a report. Another might need to understand how that report is generated and why.

**Reliable** - RIICH is for creating documentation that's practical and reliable by design. It is designed to incentivize testing, reduce friction when flagging and fixing issues, and introduce friction to making poorly thought-out changes. 

**Iterative** - The the tools we use, the services we build, and the businesses we grow are constantly updating, as are the conditions within which they operate. RIICH is designed to enrich your businesses information ecosystems no matter what stage of maturity your documentation is at, by removing  and tools for expanding and evolving existing documentation. These include consistet document structuring via templates, tools to augment and customize documentation workflows, automations that build documentation metadata and enforce structure in the background, and NLP and LLM AI tools to massively support information richness in ways previously impractical under normal business constraints.

**Inspectable** -RIICH is designed to *get out of your way* first and foremost, but at the same time it reflects my own passion for deep learning and discovery. I'm building RIICH with the understanding that often documentation is read to answer a question or solve a problem inside a time constraint.  But that path is built on a real, richly layed information environment which supports deeper inquiry and understanding without forcing it on you if all you need to do is figure out how to reboot the server for the client. 

**Composable** - Information that is documented in multiple contexts is abstracted into new document modules- reducing update-drift and documentation-overhead. RIICH is currently being prototyped in Obsidian, which allows deep customization and flexibility.  allow embedding notes inside new ones Additionally, RIICH is designed so that most of its logic can be rewritten or swapped out.  

**Honest** -  Current documentation incentive structures and technologies do not converge towards reliable output over time on their own. Time and focus are currencies that are precious in modern businesses. Simultaneously it is becoming clearer than ever to those paying attention that generative AI *cannot* be the primary driver for documenting systems where accuracy and reliability are fundamental concerns. In RIICH human authoring and generative AI use is transparent, predictable and clearly marked. One of the things I felt clearest about when I began work on RIICH is that the framework is built to empower humans to cut through and understand complexity. LLM usage absolutely supports these goals with the right harness and here it is structured, intentioned, and scoped for the actual capabilities and limitations of AI *right now*.
