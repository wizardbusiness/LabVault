I think we're at an interesting juncture in IT right now. Many companies are experimenting in real time with whether LLM's can build and mantain the complex systems that are critical for actually revenue, while cutting a lot of their engineering staff that had most of the institutional knowledge and technical experience that built these systems in the first place. Whether you see this as positive or negative disruption, it's clear to me that this is a massive gamble on the reliability of these technologies.
