
### Why should I learn anything if LLM's can do it all for me, and in less time? 


### The Issue
##### We follow tutorials to learn how to do something, but tutorials don't *teach* us how to do things. They *show* us how to do them. 
##### When we're first learning how to do something that's when having a solid foundation makes the biggest difference. It's also the point at which building a solid foundation is the most challenging. The power law distribution looks like subject^deep learning about that subject = time and frustration spent learning. 

##### Some teachers understand that a grasp of the fundamentals is deeply valuable for becoming skilled at something. Unfortunately learning the fundamentals at this stage is boring, difficult, and infuriatingly time-consuming. 

##### **Q: Why is learning the fundamentals so difficult and boring?**
#####  **A:** We're rewarded for getting *results* than for learning, even when the context is about learning.
##### Think about it- why do you want to learn something in the first place? You want to know how to **DO** something. 
##### **Q: Why would someone who cares about success want to learn anything?**
##### Because learning leads to more effective doing. Learning now is increasing your cabalility for doing  later. 

##### **What about LLM's? If the model can do what I want and in less time, why would I learn to do it myself?**
##### **A:** Thanks to LLM'S its very possible to do without learning. But by not learning you decrease your future potential to excel beyond what the model can teach you. Doing without learning is borrowing against your unrealized excellence. 

##### It used to be easy to justify learning because learning leads to success. 
### Have your cake and eat it too
##### The solution to both these problems - modern tooling not really teaching anything, and feeling like you have to choose between learning and accomplshment - is to create learning material that shows you how to do and also makes that deep learning accessible.The key word is accessible, the deep learning *cannot* be mandatory.
