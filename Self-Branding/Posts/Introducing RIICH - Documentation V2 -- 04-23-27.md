**LLM Generated Explanations**

**Reliable** - Documentation you rely on should be reliable.. A big part of this is making sure that the info you're writing down is actually good, and that it's fact-checked by someone who knows what they're actually talking about. 

**Iterative** - I've gotten overwhelmed trying to document something while working on tickets so many times and given up. Part of avoiding that is to make writing down the critical stuff as easy and frictionless as possible. Then you can fill in deeper domain knowledge over time. Documentation doesn't have to be polished or have 100% coverage to start to be useful.

**Inspectable** - You want documentation that *gets out of your way* when you're in a hurry. You want to frame the golden path for your readers. Conversely gut proficiency is gained by understanding deeper context, weird edge cases, and technical terminlogy. The way you reconcile these opposing forces is by making the guts of the topic available to the reader to inspect at their own pace without intrusively shoving those guts into their face. 

**Composable** - Information that lives in multiple contexts gets abstracted into a single source, not copy-pasted. Less duplication, less drift, less maintenance overhead.

**Honest** — You know how everything you're reading was written. AI-generated content is flagged. Human-verified content is flagged. The framework actively steers you away from using generative AI where reliability is non-negotiable.





Reliable - Critical business logic and institutional knowledge is always reviewed and verified for accuracy and completeness by an experienced human. 

Iterative - Writing documentation is tricky because the systems and processes it supports grow and change over time. Good documentation needs to anticipate this reality by being easy to iterate on while still continuously improving documentation quality. Holes and assumptions within the documentation are filled in, and out-of-date information updated. Critical information is prioritized for documentation, and future iterations flesh out context. 

Inspectable - Information is structured so as to surface critical actionable information, while using abstraction and UI design to supporting the reader to engage with supporting complexity and technical details at their own pace. 

Composable - Information is structured atomically. When information is repeated multiple times across the documentation it's extracted into its own document. Doing this makes it easier to maintain documentation and reduces documentation drift. 

Honest - LLM-generated documentation is labeled with the date and model. There is a clear boundary between what has been verified as working, and what is a work in progress.
