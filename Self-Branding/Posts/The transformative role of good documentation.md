 It may be rare, but I think transformative documentation is achievable and can be a real force multiplier on an organization's productivity and robustness. It takes time, and intention. It's not magic. The real solutions require being honest about where you're coming up short, and the technical ability to create solutions that are built on the capabilities and limitations of the tools and resources available to you. And then to take it a step further, evolving those solutions until they reach a level of excellence where it counts.These are all (or should be!) systems engineering problems, disguised as something us engineers find boring :) I'm sure i'm not the only one who's quitely felt like writing documentation is an enormous drag when you're just trying to get sh\*t done. 

 I've seen how a lack of appreciation for the role of good documentation can snowball over time and cause both chronic and acute disruption to business operations. I've also experienced how good documentation can be transformative. It can support employees in being more productive, prevent ineffeciences and mistakes, circulate critical tribal knowledge, and redefine an orgs capacity for coordination.
