##### Definition

**`Matrix`**

A rectangular grid of numbers, symbols, or expressions arranged in rows and columns that represent a mathematical object for performing calculations, storing data, or describing linear transformations between spaces.
##### ---

> [!detail]- More on `Matrix` 
> A matrix is fundamental to both pure mathematics and applied computing. In mathematics, matrices represent linear transformations and systems of equations. In computer science and machine learning, matrices are the underlying data structure for storing datasets, performing image processing, and executing neural network operations. A matrix with _m_ rows and _n_ columns is denoted as an _m × n_ matrix. Individual entries are accessed by their row and column position (e.g., _a_₁₂ means row 1, column 2).

> [!info]- _also known as_
> 
> - Rectangular array
> - 2D array (in programming)
> - Tensor of rank 2 (in advanced mathematics)
